Who are We

The Google Developer Student Club (GDSC) at the University of South Florida is one of the largest and most dynamic student organizations on campus. We are part of a global community supported by Google, dedicated to bridging the gap between theoretical knowledge and practical application in the tech world. Our mission is to empower the next generation of tech leaders by providing hands-on learning experiences, fostering innovation, and building a strong, supportive community of like-minded individuals.

What is DevFest?

Our conferences feature industry experts and thought leaders, technical workshops, a career fair attracting up to 300 students, providing valuable knowledge and fostering community connections.

Our Tracks

Expert Track

Join our Expert Track to immerse yourself in cutting-edge learning experiences with specialists in advanced tools, frameworks, artificial intelligence, software engineering, data science, cybersecurity, and emerging technologies. Whether you're mastering the latest AI innovations or diving deep into cybersecurity strategies, this track is designed to equip you with the skills and knowledge to excel. Gain exclusive insights from industry experts, empowering you to position yourself as a leader in your field.

Entrepreneurship

Attend our Entrepreneurship Track to connect with accomplished professionals who have risen to top-tier positions in the tech industry. Gain invaluable insights into the entrepreneurial mindset, learning the essential skills, qualities, and strategies that drive success in today’s competitive market. Be inspired by the personal journeys of tech visionaries, as they share their experiences overcoming challenges, seizing opportunities, and building thriving enterprises from the ground up.

Spotlight Track

Explore our Spotlight Track to engage with industry professionals as they share their early career experiences. This track offers practical advice for newcomers, covering topics such as effective job searching strategies, mastering interview techniques, and crafting standout resumes that capture the attention of hiring managers. You will also gain insights into overcoming common challenges faced when starting your career, including navigating workplace dynamics and building a professional network.

Workshop Track

Access our Workshop Track to participate in various workshops led by representatives from various clubs at USF, including Society of Hispanic Professional Engineers (SHPE), Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers in Computer Science (IEEE-CS), Brain Computer Interface club (BCI) and Society of Competitive Programmers (SCP). This track is designed to empower you to explore your interests and build a strong foundation for your future career in technology.

FAQs

I came late/I don't know where to go

No worries! If you're running late or unsure of where to go, just head to Engineering Building B (ENB). Once you get there, look for the check-in table, and our friendly team members will be there to assist you and guide you to where you need to be.

Where is Engineering Building B located?

ENB is located on the Tampa campus. It's part of the engineering complex near Leroy Collins Boulevard and Alumni Drive, close to the USF Library and the College of Engineering. The complex is easily accessible from the main USF Bull Runner shuttle routes.

Who is eligible to participate in DevFest 2024?

DevFest 2024 is open to anyone interested in technology, regardless of experience level! Whether you're a student, professional, or hobbyist, you can participate. The event typically attracts developers, designers, and tech enthusiasts who are passionate about Google technologies, software development, and innovation.

Is there a fee for DevFest 2024?

Great news! DevFest 2024 is free to attend. You can join without any fees and participate in all the sessions, workshops, and activities. Just make sure to register and secure your spot!

Who can I contact if I have any questions?

Should you have any questions, please contact us at usfgdsc@gmail.com or our President Neeraj Pillai and Vice President Minh-Ha Pham at neerajpillai@usf.edu and minhhapham@usf.edu respectively.
